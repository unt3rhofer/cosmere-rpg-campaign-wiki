    Cosmere RPG Cheatsheet

    This is unofficial fan content, created and shared for non-commercial use.
    It has not been reviewed by Dragonsteel Entertainment, LLC or Brotherwise Games, LLC.

    Combat

    Turn Types

    • Slow turn: 3 actions, 1 reaction
    • Fast turn: 2 actions, 1 reaction

    Actions

    Brace (1)

    Raise a weapon with the defensive trait or hide in cover within 5 feet of you to defend yourself against incoming attacks.

    • All attacks against you gain disadvantage as long as you keep your weapon raised or stay behind cover
    • Attacking or moving disrupts this effect
    • Defensive weapons protect from any direction
    • Cover only protects from attacks coming from the opposite side and must be substantial enough to block the attack

    Disengage (1)

    Carefully step away from an enemy.

    • Move 5 feet
    • This movement does not trigger Reactive Strikes

    Gain Advantage (1)

    Use a skill to seek an advantage over your opponent.

    • Explain how you gain advantage
    • Make a skill test using a relevant skill against the enemy’s corresponding defense
    • On success, your next test against that enemy using a different skill gains advantage

    Grapple (2)

    Attempt to restrain an opponent.

    • Make an Athletics test against the target’s Physical defense
    • On success, the target becomes Restrained
    • Ends if:
    • You become unconscious
    • You end the effect
    • The target leaves your reach

    Interact (1)

    Interact with an object within reach.

    • Usually does not require a skill test
    • Examples: opening a door, drawing or sheathing a weapon

    Move (1)

    Move up to your movement rate.

    • Crawling or moving stealthily halves movement and makes you Slowed
    • Leaping or climbing may require an Agility or Athletics test
    • Can be used multiple times per turn

    Ready (X)

    Prepare an action to trigger in response to a chosen event.

    • Choose:
    • A trigger
    • An action to perform
    • If the trigger occurs before your next turn, you may use the action as a reaction

    Recover (2)

    Take a moment to regain strength.

    • Roll your recovery die as if finishing a short rest
    • Recover health and/or focus
    • Can be used once per scene

    Strike (1)

    Make an attack against a target’s Physical defense.

    • You may Strike more than once per turn
    • Each attack must use a different weapon
    • Off-hand attacks cost 2 focus

    Graze - When you miss, you may spend 1 focus per target to deal damage equal to the rolled damage dice (no bonuses)

    Shove (2)

    Forcefully reposition an opponent.

    • Make an Athletics test vs Physical defense
    • On success, move the target 5 feet
    • If the target grappled you, the grapple ends

    Use a Skill (1)

    Use a skill to perform a challenging task (medicine, stealth, etc.).

    Reactions

    Aid (r)

    Help an ally after they roll a skill test.

    • Spend 1 focus
    • Grant advantage
    • You must be in position to realistically assist

    Avoid Danger (r)

    React to environmental danger.

    • Make an Agility test
    • DC:
    • Equal to opposing test result, or
    • 15 if no test caused the danger
    • On success, you avoid the danger

    Dodge (r)

    React to being targeted by an attack.

    • Spend 1 focus
    • Impose disadvantage on the attack
    • Cannot be used on:
    • Area attacks
    • Attacks targeting multiple characters

    Reactive Strike (r)

    Attack an enemy leaving your reach.

    • Spend 1 focus
    • Make an attack against Physical defense

    Injury Roll

    When a character suffers an injury, roll 1d20 and apply modifiers:

    • Armor: Add armor deflect value
    • Abilities: Add relevant bonuses
    • Existing Injuries: −5 per injury

    Plot Die

    Opportunities (Natural 20)

    • Aid an Ally: Next ally test gains advantage
    • Collect Yourself: Recover 1 focus
    • Critically Hit: Turn a hit into a critical hit
    • Influence the Narrative: GM-approved positive effect

    Complications (Natural 1)

    • Hinder an Ally: Next PC test gains disadvantage
    • Become Distracted: Lose 1 focus
    • Influence the Narrative: GM chooses a drawback

    Rests

    Short Rest (1 hour)

    Options: - Recover: Roll recovery die, restore health/focus - Tend to Others: Add Medicine ranks to another’s recovery

    Long Rest (8 hours)

    • Restore all health and focus
    • Reduce Exhausted penalty by 1

    Conversations

    Influence

    Roll against Cognitive defense (or Spiritual where noted).

    • Persuasion – charm and appeal
    • Deception – convincing lies
    • Intimidation – pressure and threats
    • Leadership – inspire better behavior (can target Spiritual defense)

    Help or Hinder Efforts

    • Grant an ally advantage
    • Impose disadvantage on an enemy

    Bolster an Ally

    Restore focus to an ally (amount determined by GM).

    Gather Information

    Roll against Cognitive or Spiritual defense.

    • Perception – observe suspicious behavior
    • Insight – empathize and understand motives
    • Deduction – logically dismantle arguments
